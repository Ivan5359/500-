I help contractors run a 48-hour recovery sprint: follow-up messages, segmentation, and a callback board.

Fixed $500. Want me to send the workflow?
```

Interested?
```

## 7-Minute Call

### 0:00-0:45

```text
Thanks for taking a few minutes. I will keep it simple: I am not selling ads or new leads. I help contractors recover old estimates where the customer stopped responding after the quote.
```

### 0:45-2:00

Ask:

- Do you have old estimates or no-response leads from the last 3-12 months?
- Roughly how many pile up each month?
- Who follows up with them now?
- Which jobs are most valuable: roofing, HVAC, remodel, flooring, fencing?

### 2:00-3:15

```text
Usually these leads are not all dead. Some hired someone else, some delayed the project, some forgot, and some still want help. But without a structured follow-up, they just sit there.
```

### 3:15-4:30

```text
The sprint is simple. You send 20-50 old estimates or no-response leads. In 48 hours I build the follow-up campaign and callback board. You can see who replied, who needs a call, who is not relevant, and who might still close. Fixed $500.
```

### 4:30-5:30

```text
I do not need CRM access if you do not want to give it. A spreadsheet is enough: name, contact, job type, date, quote amount if available, and notes. Everything gets approved before sending.
```

### 5:30-7:00

```text
If you have at least 20 old estimates, I would test one sprint. Worst case, you finally know which leads are dead. Best case, a few callbacks come back and one job pays for the whole thing.

Want me to send the invoice and onboarding checklist?
```

## Objections

### “We already follow up.”

```text
That is good. The question is whether the old no-response backlog also gets worked systematically. I am useful when those older estimates are not being touched.
```

### “The leads are too old.”

```text
Some will be dead. That is fine. The sprint quickly separates dead leads from people who delayed, forgot, or are still open to a callback.
```

### “$500 is too much.”

```text
I understand. The math only needs one decent job to come back. This is not a long subscription; it is one controlled test on old estimates you already have.
```

### “I do not want weird messages going to customers.”

```text
Agreed. Messages are short, normal, and approved by you. No pressure, no fake urgency, no discounts unless you approve them.
```

### “We do not have a list ready.”

```text
No problem. I send a simple template. You can export from CRM or fill in name, contact, service, quote date, and notes.
```
